An integrative approach to identifying risk factors in metabolic syndrome research -an island population model


Saša Missoni


Centre for Applied Bioanthropology, Institute for Anthropological Research, Zagreb, Croatia

Email: sasa.missoni@inantro.hr


Based on their environmental homogeneity and common practice of consanguinity, Croatian island isolates have been recognized as suitable for mapping Mendelian disease traits (Saftić et al. 2006), finding candidate genes involved in the development of different complex diseases (Rudan I. et al. 1999, 2003; Deka et al. 2008; 2012), and conducting linkage and association analyses (e.g. Vitart et al. 2008, Barbalić et al. 2009, Karns et al. 2012; 2013; Zhang et al. 2010, 2011). Expression of genes responsible for the occurrence of complex diseases is often enhanced in such isolated populations, which is why genetic-epidemiological research in population isolates gives us detailed insight into the interplay between intrinsic and extrinsic risk factors for disease development. Metabolic syndrome (MetS) and its constituents (type 2 diabetes, obesity, hypertension) have been recognized as a 21st century pandemic and a major public health concern worldwide. High prevalence of MetS has been observed in Croatian island isolates (25% and 38.5% based on Adult Treatment Panel III and International Diabetes Federation definitions, respectively) and the research results indicate that obesity, dyslipidemia, glucose intolerance, and hyperuricemia are driving elements in the development of cardiometabolic disorders in Croatian islanders. The explanation for this negative trend should be sought using an integrative approach, which takes into account the genetic, but also environmental risk factors (lifestyle, diet, socioeconomic status, toxicological burden) for disease development. This then consequently offers a path towards a population-specific, personalized approach to the problem and succesful prevention and intervention strategies.



Saša Missoni is Director of the Institute for Anthropological Research, located in Zagreb, Croatia, and Assistant Professor at the Faculty of Medicine, Josip Juraj Strossmayer University of Osijek, Croatia. He has been involved in several internationally funded projects such as Integrated GWAS and EWAS of Cardiometabolic Traits in an Island Population (National Institutes of Health, USA), Genetic and environmental factors of insulin resistance syndrome and its long term complication in immigrant Mediterranean populations (FP7), Individualized Approach in the Prediction of Type 2 Diabetes Onset and Development (European Fund for Regional Development). He was also the principal investigator of national project Croatian Islands’ Birth Cohort Study – CRIBS.
He is a Member of the Management Board of the Croatian Competitiveness Cluster for Personalized Medicine and of the Croatian Society for Human Genetics. He is also a member of the working group for the implementation of the Declaration on Genomics and representative of Ministry of Science and Education on the ICPerMed Executive Committee (International Consortium for Personalised Medicine – ICPerMed).
